Project Row Houses



We empower people and enrich communities by
creating programs and opportunities for artists,
young mothers, and small businesses in the Third Ward.

Goals

Our goal is to create a webpage that engages its users
by conveying its mission clearly, which will increase the likelihood
of user satisfaction and entices users volunteer.

The Challenge


Problem Statement

We believe that a website redesign that emphasizes the mission and community impact of Project Row Houses in narrative form will increase user engagement as well as the likelihood of users volunteering. How might we develop a website that entices our users to volunteer? My team and I were tasked to create a consistent and amazing experience across all digital ordering platforms ensuring that the webpage engages its users by conveying its mission clearly, which will increase the user satisfaction and entice users to volunteer.
Key activities included:
- User Research
- User Interviews & Key Insights
- Information Architecture
- Sketches and Prototyping



User Research

Proto Persona and User Persona

We evaluated Project Row Houses website and create a persona based what we thought would be the initial user. After our interviews, we set up a concrete persona establishing the needs, behaviors, pain points, and goals. We referred to Elijah throughout the process, particularly when designing the website.



User Testing


After many surveys and interviews, we realized that 35% of our the participants knew about Project Row Houses and most of the participants preferred donating and volunteering their time. We created a prioritization map and found that we should focus on volunteering and donating along with the mission.



Wireframes

Round 1.

We created the prototype in Adobe XD. This is the desktop view of the homepages and entering your information to donate. We wanted the homepage to be colorful like the art installations showcased. We used one of the fellowship winner’s art to pull the colors.



Round 2.

In the second iteration we started to play with navigation and experiment with color and images. We realized quickly that because the color palette is bold we needed to incorporate more white space to not overwhelm the user.



Round 3.

After more deliberation, we created a final homepage that incorporates all of core assets that were needed. When we tested our design, everyone loved the idea having multiple places to interact with the mission of the organization, volunteering, and donating.

Final Prototype



Final Thoughts

The main focus of the project was redesigning the website and really bringing life to the mission on the nonprofit. When first visiting the site, it was apparent that many of our interviewers lost interest in the website, because it didn’t depict the mission or the goal. While we were prototyping, I realized we did not build out the membership option for Project Row Houses. I do plan to build this out in the future and really give in the sister website of rowhousecdc.org.
